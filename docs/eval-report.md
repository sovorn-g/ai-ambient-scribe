# Eval Report

> The product is the deliverable; this report is the **receipt** that proves the
> stack wasn't picked by vibes. Generated by `scripts/run_bakeoff_for_report.py`
> driving the real adapters (mlx-whisper ASR → sherpa-onnx diarization →
> Ollama note-LLM) over PriMock57 mock consultations. Raw JSON +
> harness-rendered markdown: `data/.cache/bakeoff_report.{json,md}`.

## Methodology (read first)

- **No LLM-judge anywhere.** A weak local model judging a weak local model is
  circular. Every score below is a **deterministic** metric.
- **Grounding** (citation coverage + entity grounding) is the product-level
  trust mechanism — it measures whether the note is *traceable to the
  conversation*. It is the faithfulness signal.
- **Completeness** (ROUGE-1/2/L vs PriMock57 reference notes) measures *how
  much of the reference content is present*. **Completeness is never labelled
  faithfulness** (execute-plan-v2.md §8). A note can be complete and wrong.
- **WER / DER** are locked axes — ASR + diarization are model-invariant (only
  the note-LLM varies in the bake-off), so they surface once, not per model.
- A **5-note human-eyeball** sanity check is the qualitative backstop (below).

## Per-model comparison

| Model | Citation coverage ↑ | Entity grounding ↑ | ROUGE-1 ↑ | ROUGE-2 ↑ | ROUGE-L ↑ |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **qwen2.5-7b** (general 7B) | **1.0000** | 0.6001 | **0.2563** | **0.0391** | **0.1699** |
| **medgemma-4b** (medical 4B) | **1.0000** | 0.2083 | 0.1895 | 0.0100 | 0.1153 |
| **llama3.1-8b** (general 8B) | **1.0000** | **0.7696** | 0.2179 | 0.0283 | 0.1270 |

- **Citation coverage = 1.0 for all three.** `CitationValidator` is a pure,
  model-independent gate: it strips ungrounded claims and drops claims whose
  `SpanRef` doesn't resolve into the dialogue. Every surviving claim has a
  valid span citation, regardless of which LLM produced it. **The structural
  trust mechanism is model-invariant** — that's the point of keeping grounding
  deep and shared (design.md §3) instead of per-adapter.
- **Entity grounding is where the models separate** — and they separate in the
  direction the stack choice predicted.

## Interpretation — the general-vs-medical question

This is the question the bake-off exists to answer on our own data
(execute-plan-v2.md §3, §5):

> *Does a medical fine-tune (MedGemma-4B) beat a general instruct model
> (Qwen2.5-7B) at faithful clinical summarization on this pipeline?*

**No — on both axes that matter, the general 7B beats the medical 4B:**

- **Completeness:** Qwen ROUGE-1 **0.256** vs MedGemma **0.190** — the general
  model captures more of the reference note's content.
- **Entity grounding:** Qwen **0.600** vs MedGemma **0.208** — the medical
  fine-tune introduces medical entities (meds / doses / conditions) that do
  **not** appear in the transcript nearly three times as often. This is exactly
  the "medical models hallucinate more" failure mode the 2024–25 evidence
  warned about, showing up in our own deterministic metric.

**Size alone doesn't explain it.** The 8B general Llama does *not* beat the 7B
Qwen on completeness (0.218 vs 0.256), and its higher entity grounding (0.770)
comes with lower completeness — a different shape (more conservative, more
traceable, less complete). So the medical fine-tune appears to actively *hurt*
entity grounding rather than the gap being a pure parameter-count effect.

**Takeaway:** the choice of a general instruct model (Qwen2.5-7B) as the
production note-LLM is validated on our own data, and the medical fine-tune is
correctly demoted to a bake-off comparator. The trust story does not rely on
picking the "smartest" model — it relies on the grounding layer, which holds at
1.0 citation coverage for every model including the worst performer.

### Caveats

- n=6 — directional, not statistical. The shape is consistent with the
  evidence that motivated the stack; the magnitude is not a publishable claim.
- MedGemma-4B is both smaller *and* medical-fine-tuned; the two are confounded.
  The 8B Llama arm partially controls for size (above).
- ROUGE vs PriMock57's mock reference notes measures completeness against
  *another* mock note, not a gold standard — keep it labelled completeness.
- **MEDCON** (the plan's named completeness metric, execute-plan-v2.md §6) is
  **not implemented**; ROUGE-1/2/L stands in as the completeness family. MEDCON
  remains a stretch item (scope-frozen out, §10).

## 5-Note Human Eyeball Checklist

For 5 randomly sampled generated notes, manually verify:

- [ ] Every SOAP section is populated (no empty sections without clinical cause)
- [ ] No hallucinated medications, doses, or diagnoses absent from the dialogue
- [ ] Speaker attribution looks correct (CLINICIAN vs PATIENT turns)
- [ ] Citation spans are plausible and point to genuine transcript evidence
- [ ] Note reads coherently as a clinical document (no garbled or repeated text)

> The eyeball check is the qualitative backstop to the deterministic metrics.
> It is intentionally a checklist, not an LLM-judge rubric. Run it on the saved
> drafts in `data/.cache/debug_draft.json` and the SQLite `drafts.db`.

## Reproduce

```bash
# Prerequisites: ollama running, three tags pulled, scispaCy + sherpa models
# installed, PriMock57 fetched (see README.md §Quickstart).
.venv/bin/python scripts/run_bakeoff_for_report.py --n 6
# → data/.cache/bakeoff_report.json  (raw per-model metrics)
# → data/.cache/bakeoff_report.md    (harness-rendered table)
# Increase --n toward 57 for the full-dataset run (linear in n × models).
```
